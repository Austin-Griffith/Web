Since the debut of the model X it has been noted world wide that electric cars will outperform gas cars because of the instant power of electric motors. Electric Vehicle Benefits


  • Cutting Emmissions


    Since the introduction of hybrid and fully electric vehicles there has been a noticeable reduction in greenhouses gases in the areas where their are high numbers of these types of vehicles. A great example of this reduction can be seen in Los Angeles and the greater Southern California area where smog and emmissions have been a huge problem. As electric cars are trending and more and more hit the road, the reduction in emmissions and greenhouse gases will continue rise.


  • Cutting Fuel Costs


    The average driver with a gas powered car annually consumes about 6100 gallons of gas per year at an average cost of $18,000 per year (based upon cars with a average mpg of 27 and driving average of 10k miles per year). The average driver with a hybrid vehicle annually consumes about 3300 gallons of gas per year at an average cost of $9800 per year (based upon hybrid cars with an average mpg of 50 and driving 10k miles per year). The average driver with a fully electric vehicle spend no money gas but about $5200 on electricity to charge the batteries. On average electric vehicle owners save about $13,000 per year and help reduce greenhouse emmissions.


  • State and Federal Tax Cuts


    In 2011 President Barack Obama announced that hybrid and elctric car owners would recieve tax breaks as well as car manufacturers for producing and owning these types of vehciles. Seven years later we are continuing to see this trend. The tax breaks and refund amounts varies by state and brand of vehcile but are making electric cars more affordable than ever. In California there are also other incentives such as free fast-lane access and some free tolls. We are also starting to see many states building free charging stations in urban areas and most of the modern electric cars map these out.
